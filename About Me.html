Greetings,

My name is Nikos, I’m 28 years old, I live in Kallithea, Athens, Greece.

I finished the technical high school as a computer and network technician and I’m working on this field from 2012. After 2015 I decided to change my work field and learn more about programming. First I chose to study from a variety of sites like: 1) coursera.org, 2) lynda.com, 3) codeacademy.com, 4) learnhowtocode.com ….

At the same time I started making preparations in order to enroll in a university. So in 2015 I took the English proficiency exams and started sending applications in some universities abroad. After considering both the pros and cons in each case, I decided to stay here in Greece and enroll in the Hellenic Open University, in computer science.

Not long after that, I understood that even though the university could offer me some valuable knowledge, it wouldn’t teach me programming itself. So I made a new plan. This time I kept going with my uni plan but I would spend more time studying programming on my own than studying for the uni itself.

After some time, I met new people who are pursuing the same goal as me and with whom I could finally discuss about how things work on a practical level.

As I was trying to understand which specific field I was more suited to, I did a variety of courses like: Python seminars. Html, CSS, Bootstrap, Dreamweaver, JavaScript, DOM manipulation, jQuerry, CMS (WordPress), Git, C (both in and out of the uni), SQL and this period of time I’m learning Java and its frameworks (Spring, Maven, Hibernate).

Previous job In my previous job I was working as a computer and network technician in a private company, in which, except my technical responsibilities I also had some programming duties. As a technician I was offering tech services on the applications of the company (ERP programs). I was creating private networks for the companies with which we had contracts. We were working mostly with Microsoft’s products. MS SQL, etc.. From April 2018 I managed to enter the programming department of the company and was working on an accounting program. There I saw C#, .Net, MS SQL.
